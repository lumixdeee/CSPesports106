# Story Domains Covered by the Framework

This document enumerates story domains understood by the framework.
A *story domain* is any medium or state in which meaning unfolds across time,
whether internally generated, externally mediated, embodied, symbolic, or interactive.

The framework does not claim ownership of these domains.
It provides lenses and tools that may be applied where useful.

---

## 0. Observed Reality (Primary Substrate)

This domain precedes all narrative formation.
It is not story, but the source from which story may later emerge.

---

### 0A. Raw Scene (External Field)

- Continuous external environment
- Physical objects, forces, and events
- Independent of observer
- No segmentation, no narrative structure
- Exists regardless of perception

---

### 0B. Sensory Intake (Transduction Layer)

- Light → vision
- Air vibration → sound
- Chemical signals → smell / taste
- Pressure / temperature → touch
- Body state → interoception

- Partial, filtered, lossy capture of the raw scene
- Constrained by sense organs and position
- Still pre-narrative

---

### 0C. Perceptual Selection (Pre-Narrative Mind)

- Attention selects from sensory intake
- Feature binding (edges, motion, objects)
- Salience detection (threat, novelty, relevance)
- Temporal chunking begins

- Forms proto-units (object, movement, change)
- Not yet story, but structured perception

---

### 0D. Mind Story (Internal Narrative Formation)

- Sequencing of perceived events
- Causality attribution
- Agent identification
- Emotional tagging
- Compression into symbolic or semi-symbolic form

- This is where “story” begins internally

---

### 0E. Interpretation (Meta-Understanding Layer)

- Reflection on the mind story
- Meaning assignment
- Integration with memory and beliefs
- Abstraction, generalisation, explanation

- Produces:
  - conclusions
  - lessons
  - models
  - retellable narratives

---

## Properties of Domain 0

- Continuous → discrete transition occurs across layers
- Bandwidth reduces at each stage
- Information is filtered, not added
- Narrative emerges only at 0D+
- All higher domains depend on outputs from this pipeline

---

## Relation to Other Domains

- Domain I (Internal) operates primarily on:
  - 0D (mind story)
  - 0E (interpretation)

- Domains II–VIII (symbolic / mediated) encode:
  - outputs of 0D and 0E

- Domain IX (embodied / enacted):
  - feeds back into 0A (raw scene)
  - cycles through the pipeline again

---

## Boundary Condition

- 0A–0C: not narrative
- 0D: narrative begins
- 0E: narrative becomes explicit, transferable, and analysable

---

## Summary Flow

Raw Scene (0A)
→ Sensory Intake (0B)
→ Perceptual Selection (0C)
→ Mind Story (0D)
→ Interpretation (0E)
→ (feeds all other domains)

- Intrusive imagery
- Flashbacks

# Domains Explicitly Out of Interfacing Scope

The following are **valid semantic domains**, but the framework does not
directly interface with them. These domains are constrained, deterministic,
or optimised for correctness rather than meaning.

The framework may describe, document, or metaphorically reference them,
but does not claim operational authority within them.

---

## A. Purely Mechanical Domains

- Arithmetic
- Logic proofs
- Checksums
- Cryptographic primitives
- Hashing and encoding primitives

---

## B. Fully Constrained and Deterministic Systems

- Low-level protocols
- Signal encoding specifications
- Clocked systems
- State machines
- Hardware timing and sequencing
- Formal grammars used as execution constraints

---

## C. Precision-First Verification Domains

- Measurement and calibration
- Timing analysis
- Formal verification
- Safety-critical validation
- Proof systems where ambiguity is an error

---

These domains are respected, not absorbed.

Bridges, visualisations, metaphors, and educational layers may exist
as optional, downstream work, but correctness sovereignty remains local.
